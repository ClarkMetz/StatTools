Probability Distributions Overview

Distribution Description PDF/PMF Equation General Use Case Detailed Example
BetaA continuous distribution defined on the interval \( [0, 1] \), often used to model probabilities and proportions. Controlled by two shape parameters \( \alpha \) and \( \beta \).\( f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \frac{x^{\alpha - 1}(1 - x)^{\beta - 1}}{B(\alpha, \beta)} \), for \( 0 < x < 1 \)Modeling proportions and probabilities.In digital marketing, an advertiser may estimate the probability that a user will click on an ad using a Beta distribution, updating beliefs as more data comes in.
Chi-SquareA continuous distribution commonly used in hypothesis testing and confidence interval estimation for variance and categorical data.\( f(x; k) = \frac{1}{2^{k/2} \Gamma(k/2)} x^{k/2 - 1} e^{-x/2} \), for \( x > 0 \)Analyzing categorical data and variability.In a genetics study, a chi-square test can determine if the observed distribution of traits matches the expected Mendelian ratio.
CauchyA continuous distribution with heavy tails and undefined mean and variance. It arises in ratios of normally distributed variables.\( f(x; x_0, \gamma) = \frac{1}{\pi \gamma \left[1 + \left(\frac{x - x_0}{\gamma}\right)^2\right]} \)Modeling resonance and peak effects.In spectroscopy, the Cauchy distribution can model the shape of spectral lines affected by resonance broadening.
ExponentialModels the time between independent events occurring at a constant rate. Memoryless property.\( f(x; \lambda) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x},\ x \ge 0 \)Modeling time until an event occurs.A call center may use the exponential distribution to model the time between incoming customer support calls.
FA ratio of two scaled chi-square distributions, used primarily in analysis of variance (ANOVA).\( f(x; d_1, d_2) = \frac{(d_1 x)^{d_1/2} d_2^{d_2/2}}{x (d_1 x + d_2)^{(d_1 + d_2)/2} B(d_1/2, d_2/2)} \)Comparing variability across groups.In an educational study, an F-test may compare student test score variances across different teaching methods.
GammaA two-parameter family of continuous distributions useful for modeling waiting times.\( f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \frac{\beta^\alpha x^{\alpha - 1} e^{-\beta x}}{\Gamma(\alpha)} \)Modeling positive continuous data with skew.An insurance company uses the Gamma distribution to model the size and frequency of claims filed in a given period.
LognormalA distribution of a variable whose logarithm is normally distributed.\( f(x; \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{x \sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(\ln x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \), \( x > 0 \)Modeling skewed data with a lower bound.Economists may use the lognormal distribution to estimate the distribution of household incomes within a region.
NormalA symmetric, bell-shaped distribution characterized by its mean and standard deviation.\( f(x; \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}} e^{-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}} \)Modeling symmetric natural variation.In quality control, the normal distribution models the variability in manufacturing dimensions of a machined part.
T-DistributionA distribution used instead of the normal distribution when sample sizes are small and population standard deviation is unknown.\( f(t; \nu) = \frac{\Gamma((\nu+1)/2)}{\sqrt{\nu \pi} \Gamma(\nu/2)} \left(1 + \frac{t^2}{\nu}\right)^{-(\nu+1)/2} \)Estimating means from small samples.A researcher uses the t-distribution to construct a confidence interval for the mean height of a plant species based on a small sample of 10 measurements.
UniformA distribution where all outcomes are equally likely over a certain interval \( [a, b] \).\( f(x) = \frac{1}{b - a},\ a \le x \le b \)Modeling equally likely outcomes.In simulations, software may generate random times between tasks using a uniform distribution between 1 and 5 minutes.
WeibullA flexible distribution used for modeling lifetimes and failure rates.\( f(x; k, \lambda) = \frac{k}{\lambda} \left(\frac{x}{\lambda}\right)^{k - 1} e^{-(x/\lambda)^k} \)Modeling life durations and reliability.An engineer models the lifetime of ball bearings in an industrial machine using the Weibull distribution to predict maintenance schedules.
